While things get sorted out

Ways to protect yourself, starting today

Reporting starts the process — these are things you can do right now, on your own, to feel safer in the meantime.

In person Staying safe day to day +
  • Stay near friends or a teacher during the times or places it usually happens — bullying is less likely when others are around.
  • It's okay to walk away without responding. Leaving isn't losing.
  • Agree on a signal with a friend so they know when to step in or get an adult.
  • Avoid retaliating physically — it can put you at risk even when you're the one being targeted.
Online Protecting yourself digitally +
  • Set your profiles to private and remove people who make you uncomfortable — you don't owe anyone access.
  • Block and mute instead of replying. Engaging often escalates things.
  • Don't delete the messages — screenshot them first, even the upsetting ones. They're proof.
  • Never share passwords, even with close friends.
Records Keeping a paper trail +
  • Jot down the date, time, place, and what was said or done, as soon as you can after it happens.
  • Save screenshots to a folder only you can access, not just your camera roll.
  • Note any witnesses — people who saw it happen can support your report.
  • Keep records even after reporting; patterns matter if it continues.
Wellbeing Looking after how you feel +
  • What's happening to you is not something you caused or deserve.
  • It's normal to feel angry, embarrassed, or numb — all of those are okay.
  • Talk to someone you trust about how it's affecting you, not just what happened.
  • If it's affecting your sleep, appetite, or you're feeling unsafe with yourself, tell an adult right away — that matters as much as the bullying itself.

Common questions

Before you report

Will my parents automatically be told? +
Not automatically. Staff will usually talk with you first about who needs to know and when — except in situations where your safety requires involving a parent or guardian right away.
Can I really stay anonymous? +
Yes. The anonymous toggle is on by default. If you leave it on, no name, login, or device information is attached to your report.
What if I'm not sure it counts as "bullying"? +
Report it anyway. You don't need to label it correctly — if it made you feel unsafe, targeted, or upset, it's worth telling someone.
What happens after I submit a report? +
A staff member reviews it, usually within one school day, and decides on next steps — which may include checking in with you first, quietly, before anything else happens.
